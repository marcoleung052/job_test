§H3§ Overview
The IO Expander connects directly to the XDAQ unit, allowing users to monitor and control additional signals efficiently. The digital and analog channels provide precise input and output operations, making it ideal for real-time experimental setups requiring expanded signal processing.
§H3§ Key Features
Synchronized IO Processing:
Operates at the same sampling rate as the X-Headstage.
Compact and Robust Design:
Easily integrates with the existing XDAQ system without extensive modifications.

§H3§ Device Overview
Expanded Digital I/O
Adds 24 digital input channels and 24 digital output channels to the XDAQ, enabling flexible control and monitoring of external devices.
Wide-Range Analog I/O
Provides 6 analog input channels (ADC) and 6 analog output channels (DAC) with wide voltage ranges for interfacing with diverse laboratory equipment.
Synchronized Operation
Digital and analog I/O operations are synchronized with the XDAQ system timing for precise, deterministic signal control.
Modular System Expansion
Seamlessly integrates with the XDAQ platform, allowing scalable I/O expansion without modifying the core acquisition system.
Versatile Experimental Integration
Supports a wide range of experimental setups, including behavioral control, stimulation triggering, and external device synchronization.
§H3§ Hardware Connection
§H4§ Connect the IO Expander to the XDAQ via the EXP1 and EXP2 ports using the supplied cables.
